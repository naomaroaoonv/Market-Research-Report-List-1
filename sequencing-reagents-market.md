<p><p>Sequencing reagents are essential for various DNA sequencing technologies, which include Sanger sequencing, Next Generation Sequencing (NGS), and Third Generation Sequencing (TGS). Sanger sequencing, known for its accuracy, employs chain-terminating inhibitors for sequencing short DNA fragments. NGS allows massive parallel sequencing, making it efficient for large genomes. TGS offers real-time sequencing with longer read lengths, enabling more comprehensive analysis of complex genomes. Each market targets distinct applications, from clinical diagnostics to research, driving advancements in genomics.</p></p>
