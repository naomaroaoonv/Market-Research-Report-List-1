<p><p>The implantable biomaterial market encompasses various types, each serving distinct medical applications. Metals and metal alloys, known for durability and biocompatibility, are commonly used in orthopedic and dental implants. Synthetic polymers offer versatility and tailored properties for soft tissue applications. Ceramics provide excellent wear resistance and biocompatibility, ideal for bone-related implants. Natural biomaterials, derived from biological sources, mimic native tissue properties, enhancing integration. Composites combine different materials to optimize performance, addressing specific mechanical and biological requirements in implant design.</p></p>
