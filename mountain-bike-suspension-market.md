<p><p>Mountain bike suspension systems are categorized into front and rear suspension types, each serving distinct purposes. Front suspension, typically utilizing a fork, enhances control and comfort by absorbing impacts from the terrain while allowing better steering. Rear suspension, often featuring either a full-suspension or hardtail design, provides additional traction and stability, improving overall ride quality on rough trails. Together, these systems help riders navigate challenging landscapes, making the suspension market essential for enhancing performance and rider experience in mountain biking.</p></p>
