<p><p>Carbon thermoplastic composites are categorized mainly into two types based on the carbon fiber precursor: PAN (Polyacrylonitrile) and Pitch. PAN-based composites are known for their high strength, stiffness, and thermal stability, making them suitable for aerospace and automotive applications. In contrast, Pitch-based composites offer superior thermal and electrical conductivity, often utilized in electronic and sporting goods. Both types leverage the lightweight, durable characteristics of thermoplastics, enhancing performance and efficiency across diverse industries.</p></p>
