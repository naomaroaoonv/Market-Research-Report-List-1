<p><p>Chip packaging involves encasing semiconductor devices to protect them and enhance performance, facilitating integration into electronic systems. Major types include die bonding, wire bonding, flip-chip, and system-in-package (SiP) methods, each optimizing size, thermal management, and signal integrity. Meanwhile, testing ensures reliability and functionality through various methods, including wafer testing, package testing, and system testing. This process identifies defects and validates performance, ensuring chips meet industry standards for performance and reliability before deployment in end applications.</p></p>
