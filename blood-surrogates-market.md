<p><p>Blood surrogates are innovative alternatives to traditional blood transfusions, primarily categorized into two markets: HBOC (Hemoglobin-Based Oxygen Carriers) and PFBOC (Perfluorocarbon-Based Oxygen Carriers). HBOCs utilize modified hemoglobin to transport oxygen, addressing supply shortages and compatibility issues, while PFBOCs leverage perfluorocarbon compounds to dissolve and transport oxygen in the bloodstream, offering potential benefits in various medical scenarios. Both markets aim to enhance oxygen delivery and improve patient outcomes in emergencies and surgical settings, representing significant advancements in medical technology.</p></p>
